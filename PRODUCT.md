# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo users (and optionally one added household profile — a partner, not a separate login) tracking personal debt, income, expenses, and payments to build a payoff plan. Context: checking in regularly (weekly/monthly) on real numbers that carry financial stress; needs to scan data fast and trust it, not decode it.

## Product Purpose

DebtClear turns debt payoff into a trackable, motivating plan: log debts/income/expenses/payments, see progress, follow a payoff strategy. Originally a personal Base44 app for the founder and his wife, now being rebuilt as a public product. Success = users can see their numbers clearly at a glance and feel forward momentum, not shame or confusion. A public-facing marketing homepage (`src/components/home/*`) sits in front of the app and is evaluated under brand principles secondarily; the app screens (dashboard, debts, expenses, income, payments, payoff-plan) are the primary, everyday-use surface.

## Brand Personality

Friendly, optimistic fintech — Wise-inspired (lime-green accent, sage/ink neutrals, generous rounded cards). Calm confidence, not clinical-accounting-cold and not guilt/doom-based debt-shaming. Debt is a sensitive topic; the tone should feel like a capable friend, not a spreadsheet or a scold.

## Anti-references

- Not a bare spreadsheet / cold enterprise-accounting UI.
- Not a guilt-trip / doom-metric debt app (red-everywhere, shame-driven).
- Not over-decorated fintech marketing kitsch that undercuts trust in real money data.

## Design Principles

1. Numbers must be scannable at a glance — this is a checked-in-on-frequently utility, not a browsed magazine.
2. Legible before pretty — secondary/label text still has to be read comfortably, not just imply hierarchy.
3. Calm, encouraging tone over alarm — use semantic color (positive/warning/negative) deliberately, don't default to red/mute-everything.
4. One accent color (brand lime-green) stays reserved for primary actions/progress, not diluted into decoration.
5. App screens are static/utility (no marketing motion); the homepage can carry more brand expression.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target (confirmed by user, 2026-07-14): body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text/UI components ≥3:1. No other stated accommodations.
